SoRSS — Soft Robotic Gastric Simulator

Hashem, R. et al. — Soft Robotics (2022)

SoRSS is a fully soft, anatomically realistic robotic simulator of the human stomach, developed to reproduce gastric anatomy and antral motility for in-vitro experimentation and medical device testing. The platform enables controlled, repeatable studies of peristaltic waves, lumen occlusion and pressure dynamics under varied conditions.

  • Problem: Few controllable, repeatable experimental platforms exist for studying gastric motility and device evaluation.
  • Approach: Distributed ring-shaped soft pneumatic actuators and bellows units arranged in zones to emulate fundus, antrum and pylorus behaviour.
  • Validation: Pressure sensing and video-based measures show realistic contraction waves and occlusion ratios across tested conditions.
  • Domain: Soft robotics · biomimetic organs · gastrointestinal modelling · medical device testing.
