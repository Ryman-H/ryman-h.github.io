SoRSS — Soft Robotic Gastric Simulator

Hashem, R. et al. — Soft Robotics (2022)

SoRSS is a fully soft, anatomically realistic robotic simulator of the human stomach, developed to replicate gastric anatomy and peristaltic motility for in-vitro experimentation and medical device testing.

  • Problem: Lack of controllable, repeatable platforms for studying gastric motility and digestion.
  • Approach: Distributed ring-shaped soft pneumatic actuators enabling travelling antral contraction waves.
  • Outcome: Experimentally validated gastric motility and pressure profiles under different conditions.
  • Domain: Soft robotics, biomimetic organs, gastrointestinal modelling, device evaluation.
