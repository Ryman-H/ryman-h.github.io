Proprioceptive Glove — Haptic Feedback for Force-Regulation Training

Soft-robotic pneumatic glove system — proprioceptive haptic feedback for grip-force training

A soft robotic glove paired with an instrumented racket handle was developed to provide magnitude-specific proprioceptive haptic feedback for grip-force regulation training. The platform integrates a six-axis load cell, surface EMG, and a pneumatic glove whose chamber pressures are modulated in real time. The system delivers adaptive, assistance-on-demand haptic cues (and visual alternatives) to guide users toward target grip magnitudes while preserving natural proprioceptive cues and motor autonomy.   • Problem: Training continuous grip-force regulation (e.g., in racket sports or rehabilitation) is limited by visual feedback approaches that increase cognitive load and produce poor retention when cues are removed.
  • Approach: Measure whole-hand force with an embedded six-axis load cell, capture forearm EMG (flexor/extensor), and render magnitude-specific proprioceptive feedback through a pneumatic glove. Feedback modes include ANN-based haptic guidance, spring/damper haptics, and visual line-graph displays.
  • Validation: A case study showed haptic feedback produced slower initial learning but better retention relative to visual feedback (training RMSE reduction ≈39% and retention errors maintained ≈20% of target). Calibration experiments characterize grip styles, pressure→force coefficients, and EMG→force regression (R² ≈ 0.89, RMSE ≈ 22.24 N for the regression).
