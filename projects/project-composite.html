Composite Stretchable Sensors

D. Hardman, R. Hashem, F. Iida — RoboSoft 2023.

Stretchable composite sensors (hydrogel–silicone channels and composite skins) designed to detect large asymmetric deformations in soft manipulators. These sensors provide robust proprioceptive signals (finger position/shape) and exteroceptive cues (object contact / asymmetry). :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

  • Problem: Soft manipulators lack reliable integrated sensing that survives large strains and gives actionable signals for control.
  • Approach: Embed stretchable composite channels (hydrogel/silicone) within manipulator skins and use shaped placements to detect bifurcation/asymmetric deformation.
  • Validation: Tensile and cyclic testing, delamination and survivability checks, and integration into a soft manipulator demonstration with clear detection of asymmetric buckling events.
  • Outcome: Sensors sustain large strains (>200%), detect asymmetry reliably, and can be used for simple threshold-based control strategies (slip/bifurcation detection).

Practical notes

  • Place sensors across expected bending/buckling regions and route leads away from high-strain anchor points.
  • Use thresholded or transient detection before moving to full state-estimators — this gives a fast route to robustness.
  • Good fit for grippers that must detect shape/offset of objects or for hands that need cheap, embedded proprioception.
