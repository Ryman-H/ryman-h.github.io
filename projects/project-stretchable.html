Wearable Stretchable Sensors

L. Cha, R. Hashem et al. — RoboSoft / Wearable Robotics

A wearable sensing platform based on stretchable, textile-integrated strain sensors designed for joint tracking in real-world movement. The work focuses on robust fabrication, comfort, and repeatability rather than laboratory-only sensing, targeting applications in sports science, rehabilitation, and injury prevention.

  • Problem: Wearable sensors often fail outside the lab due to fragile wiring, poor textile integration, and motion artefacts.
  • Approach: Multi-material direct ink writing of stretchable resistive sensors directly onto fabric, combined with automated embroidery for mechanical anchoring and electrical interconnects.
  • Validation: Bench testing and on-body experiments on the elbow and knee, demonstrating repeatable joint-angle tracking during dynamic motion.
  • Outcome: A scalable fabrication route for washable, comfortable wearable sensors suitable for sports monitoring and human movement analysis.

Applications

  • Sports performance analysis and training feedback
  • Rehabilitation and return-to-play monitoring
  • Wearable systems for injury prevention and load management
