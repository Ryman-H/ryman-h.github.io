Stretchable Wearable Sensors for Joint Tracking

Cha, L.; Hashem, R. — RoboSoft

This project develops stretchable, textile-integrated strain sensors for wearable monitoring of joint motion, enabling robust measurement of elbow and knee kinematics during movement and physical activity.

  • Problem: Wearable strain sensors often suffer from fragile electrical connections and poor mechanical integration with textiles, limiting durability and repeatability in real-world use.
  • Approach: A hybrid fabrication process combining multi-material direct ink writing with automated embroidery to integrate stretchable conductive sensors directly into fabric substrates.
  • Validation: Mechanical testing and on-body experiments demonstrate high stretchability, linear strain response, and reliable joint-angle tracking during elbow and knee motion.
  • Domain: Wearable sensing · soft electronics · textile integration · sports and rehabilitation monitoring.
