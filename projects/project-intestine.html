SoLi — Soft Robotic Large Intestine

R. Hashem, A. Escobar, E. Devlin, A. Stilli & D. Stoyanov — (project)

SoLi is a modular soft-robotic phantom that reproduces the anatomy and dynamic motility of the large intestine for training and device testing. Using independently actuated silicone ring (haustral) units, SoLi generates physiologically inspired peristalsis and segmentation patterns while supporting pressure monitoring, FEA validation and controlled transport experiments. The platform is intended for colonoscopy training, device validation, and research into GI biomechanics under repeatable, observable conditions.

  • Problem: Existing colonoscopy phantoms are mostly static or provide limited mechanical response and cannot reproduce the large intestine’s active motility (peristalsis, segmentation) needed for realistic procedure training and device evaluation.
  • Approach: SoLi uses modular hollow silicone ring actuators (haustra) cast from 3D-printed moulds, each tube independently pneumatically driven and arranged to match anatomical curvature. The design and material properties were tuned with finite-element analysis (Ogden hyperelastic model) to achieve realistic deformation and lumen dynamics.
  • Validation: The system was tested with sequential inflation patterns to demonstrate forward mass movement of test payloads, internal pressure verification to ensure physiological ranges, viscosity-variation transport trials, and durability cycling to assess longevity.
  • Platform: The electropneumatic control stack comprises proportional regulators, vacuum generators and multiplexed valves driven from MATLAB/Simulink via an Arduino interface to allow precise timing, bidirectional pressure control, and flexible motility programs.
  • Domain: Surgical training · soft-robotic organ phantoms · device evaluation · biomechanics.
Due completion in April 2026.
