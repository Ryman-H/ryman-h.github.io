Soft Robotic Large Intestine Simulator

Ongoing project — soft robotic organ phantoms & medical training

This project develops a soft robotic simulator of the human large intestine, designed to reproduce colonic anatomy and motility for device testing and medical training. The platform enables controlled, repeatable experiments under dynamic intestinal motion that cannot be captured by static phantoms.

  • Problem: Existing colon phantoms are static and cannot reproduce realistic motility or patient variability.
  • Approach: Modular soft pneumatic actuators arranged to generate segmental contraction and propagating peristalsis.
  • Simulation: Finite element analysis used to guide actuator geometry, material selection, and deformation limits.
  • Training focus: Integration with generative models to create variable, patient-specific training scenarios.
  • Applications: Colonoscopy training, device evaluation, and studies of colonic transport and interaction.
