Low-Cost High-Performance MR-Safe Pneumatic Stepper Motor

Z. Liang, R. Hashem et al. — MR-Safe Actuation for Medical Robotics

A compact, low-cost, and high-performance pneumatic stepper motor designed for operation inside MRI environments. The motor provides precise, repeatable motion while meeting strict MR-safety constraints, enabling robotic systems for MRI-guided interventions without electromagnetic interference.

  • Problem: MRI-guided robotic interventions require actuators that are non-magnetic, non-conductive, compact, and reliable — while still delivering useful torque and positioning accuracy.
  • Approach: A direct-drive pneumatic stepper motor using standard commercial syringes as pistons and cylinders, combined with 3D-printed MR-safe components. This design improves airtightness, torque output, and manufacturability while keeping costs low.
  • Validation: Bench-top characterisation of torque, air leakage, positional repeatability, and shaft stability, alongside testing inside a clinical MRI scanner to verify minimal image interference during operation.
  • Outcome: A compact MR-safe motor achieving high torque for its size, negligible air leakage, accurate stepping behaviour, and reliable operation inside a 3T MRI — suitable as a core actuator for MRI-compatible robotic systems.

Applications

  • MRI-guided needle insertion and interventional robotics
  • MR-compatible positioning stages and linear rails
  • Medical robots operating in confined, high-field environments
