RoSE — Robotic Soft Esophagus (RoSEv2.0)

Bhattacharya, D.; Hashem, R.; Cheng, L. K.; Xu, W. — IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics (2022)

RoSEv2.0 (Robotic Soft Esophagus) is an instrumented, anatomically inspired soft-robotic conduit developed to provide a reproducible in-vitro platform for studying esophageal peristalsis and for testing endoprosthetic stents under physiologically relevant bolus-swallow conditions. The device combines a multi-layer pneumatic soft conduit, embedded time-of-flight and pressure sensing, and a model-predictive control (MPC) system based on sparse identification of nonlinear dynamics (SINDYc) to generate and accurately track prescribed peristaltic waveforms for migration testing and transport experiments. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

  • Problem: In vitro stent testing and quantitative studies of esophageal transport require a platform that reproduces realistic geometry, dynamic peristalsis, and measurable state feedback — capabilities lacking in prior open-loop test rigs.
  • Approach: RoSEv2.0 uses a 12-layer soft pneumatic conduit with local whorls of chambers, external PDMS casing with an array of TOF (distance) sensors and valve pressure sensors, and an MPC controller built on discrete-time SINDYc models to synthesize time-shifted peristaltic waves.
  • Validation: The MPC produced repeatable peristaltic waves (example: 20 mm·s⁻¹ speed, 75 mm wavelength, amplitudes 5–10 mm), enabled closed-loop determination of valve/pressure commands for full lumen occlusion, and was used to run stent migration tests with varying bolus viscosities.
  • Domain: Soft robotics · continuum actuators · data-driven control (SINDYc + MPC) · in-vitro medical device testing.
