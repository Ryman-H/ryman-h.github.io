MR-SLTS — Mixed Reality Sensorised Laryngoscope Training System

The Mixed Reality Sensorised Laryngoscope Training System (MR-SLTS) couples a sensorised laryngoscope with head-mounted holographic feedback to provide real-time visualisation of contact forces and torques during paediatric endotracheal intubation training. By presenting low-latency, in-situ torque feedback in the operator’s field of view, the system reduces attention shifts to external monitors and guides trainees to keep applied forces within expert-defined safe ranges while preserving procedural efficiency.

  • Problem: Novices commonly apply excessive force or torque during laryngoscopy; conventional manikin training with verbal feedback lacks quantitative, real-time guidance to correct this safely.
  • Approach: MR-SLTS instruments a standard laryngoscope with F/T sensing and IMU, streams processed z-torque to a desktop server, and renders holographic feedback (bar, ring, or line graph formats) via HoloLens 2 with synchronized eye-gaze logging.
  • Validation: Two user studies (sensorimotor task and full paediatric ETI) showed that bar-graph holograms minimized tracking error and that trainees receiving MR torque feedback maintained forces/torques closer to expert ranges without increasing intubation time.
  • Platform: NI DAQ + Arduino data acquisition → MATLAB processing → TCP streaming → Unity/MRTK holograms on HoloLens 2; QR-code spatial registration and eye-gaze telemetry enable aligned, low-latency feedback and analysis.
  • Domain: Medical simulation · mixed reality · sensorised tools · training & assessment for airway management.
