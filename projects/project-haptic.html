HaptiSuit — Modular Dual-Mode Haptic Actuator

Oliver Wing; Ryman Hashem; Liang He — Institute of Biomedical Engineering, University of Oxford

HaptiSuit is a modular, full-body haptic system that combines high-frequency vibrotactile actuation with low-frequency pneumatic pressure in compact Vibro-Pneumatic Units (VPUs). Each VPU integrates an ERM vibromotor mounted on a rigid plate and four inflatable silicone air cells, enabling three feedback modes, vibration, sustained pressure, and a composite of both to broaden the tactile palette available in VR, training, and rehabilitation scenarios. The architecture uses a primary-secondary microcontroller topology for low-latency, scalable control across many VPUs, and the VPU design is validated via FEA, bench characterisation, and preliminary human discrimination studies.

  • Problem: Current wearable haptic systems typically provide either vibration or pneumatic pressure but not both; this limits the range of realistic sensations (impact, texture, sustained force) and reduces immersion for applications that require both transient and sustained cues.
  • Approach: Design a compact Vibro-Pneumatic Unit (64×64×8 mm) cast in Ecoflex with an embedded PLA mounting plate for an ERM motor and four 10 mm air cells. Control is implemented with a Teensy primary controller communicating over I²C to secondary controllers for vibration (DRV2605 drivers via a multiplexer) and pneumatic regulation (proportional regulators + pressure sensors).
  • Validation: FEA compared flat vs convex air-cell geometries and informed a convex design for even displacement and reduced hysteresis. Bench tests show force outputs up to ~7 N (safe operating range 20–50 kPa), reliable vibration up to ~200 Hz, and distinct discrimination of combined pressure/vibration patterns in a small participant study (location/intensity identification reported in the paper).
  • Platform: Modular VPUs mounted on a neoprene brace (or distributed garment), PC GUI for experiment control, and MATLAB/firmware stack for real-time streaming and logging. The design emphasises low cost, scalability, and easy reconfiguration for full-body or localized testing.
  • Domain: Wearable haptics · virtual reality · rehabilitation and training · multisensory interfaces.
