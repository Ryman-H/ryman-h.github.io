The Xeno-Tongue Gripper

K. Gilday, R. Hashem, A. Abdulali, F. Iida — RoboSoft 2023.

A morphing granular-jamming suction cup augmented by a small vacuum-driven bellows (“tongue”) that actively preforms and adapts the jamming surface to diverse object geometries. The tongue reduces the need for precise contact and expands usable grasp envelopes for soft suction-based end-effectors. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

  • Problem: Suction cups struggle with textured/offset surfaces and irregular shapes; forming a reliable seal is often the limiting step.
  • Approach: Add a small bellows that exerts an initial pull/press on the granular packing to conform the cup to the target, improving seal formation before jamming.
  • Validation: Bench tests and pick trials on a variety of object shapes and textures; analytical and empirical models for bellows/jamming behaviour establish operating envelopes.
  • Outcome: Broader object coverage, higher pick success on irregular objects, and a modular tongue module concept for rapid end-effector adaptation.

Practical notes

  • Package the tongue + cup as a swappable module sized to object class (small / medium / large).
  • Use the tongue as a pre-seal step in a simple finite-state controller (attempt→seal→lift) to get reliable behaviour quickly.
  • Good candidate for delicate agricultural picking where surface uncertainty is high.
