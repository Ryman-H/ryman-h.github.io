Soft Robotic Gripper — Xeno-Tongue Adaptive Jamming + Embedded Composite Sensors

K. Gilday, R. Hashem, A. Abdulali, F. Iida — (Xeno-Tongue, RoboSoft 2023); D. Hardman, R. Hashem, F. Iida — (Composite Stretchable Sensors, RoboSoft 2023)

This project pairs a novel adaptive suction-gripper design (the Xeno-Tongue: a granular-jamming suction cup augmented with a small bellow “tongue”) with soft, stretchable composite sensors integrated into soft manipulators. The combined system addresses robust grasping across varied object geometries and real-world environments while providing embedded proprioceptive and exteroceptive sensing for closed-loop control and object-aware behaviours.

  • Problem: Conventional suction cups and soft grippers struggle with textured or offset surfaces, cluttered environments, and lack embedded sensing for robust closed-loop manipulation.
  • Approach: Xeno-Tongue: a morphing granular-jamming cup augmented with a vacuum-driven bellows (“tongue”) that provides an initial pulling force to adapt the cup to objects (reducing environmental constraints for forming a seal). Composite sensors: hydrogel–silicone stretchable channels cast into the skin of soft manipulators to detect large strains and asymmetric deformations (used for bifurcation sensing and object-aware control).
  • Validation: The gripper is characterised with analytical models and bench tests (bellows force, sealing/pressing force envelopes, freestanding grasp experiments) and evaluated on benchmarking surfaces and diverse real objects (fruits, packets, etc.). The composite sensors are validated with tensile/cyclic tests, morphology studies, and integration into an underactuated soft manipulator for both proprioception (finger position) and exteroception (object shape/size/asymmetry).
  • Key results: Xeno-Tongue: successful picks across a wide range of shapes and weights (objects ≥ ~17 mm), predictable operating envelopes from combined bellows/jamming models, and strong performance on smooth and many textured objects with reduced reliance on reaction forces. Composite sensors: stretchable hydrogel–silicone channels survive >200% strain before delamination, detect bifurcation direction reliably, and enable simple thresholded and transient-based closed-loop control strategies.
  • Applications: Agri-robotic harvesting and handling, soft grippers for unstructured pick-and-place, sensorised soft manipulators for in-hand manipulation and tactile-aware control, and experimental platforms for embodied intelligence in soft robotics.
