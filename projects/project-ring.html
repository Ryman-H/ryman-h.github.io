A Biologically Inspired Ring-Shaped Soft Pneumatic Actuator (RiSPA)

R. Hashem, S. Kazemi, M. Stommel, L. K. Cheng, W. Xu — Soft Robotics, 2022

RiSPA is a ring-shaped soft pneumatic actuator designed to mimic the radial contractions of the gastrointestinal tract (notably the stomach antrum). Constructed from multiple bellows-style SPAs embedded in a soft ring frame with integrated range sensors, RiSPA achieves large occlusion ratios and supports both symmetrical and asymmetrical contraction patterns suitable for bio-inspired peristaltic tasks, sensing-enabled control, and organ-simulator testbeds.

  • Design: A ring frame houses five bellows-driven soft pneumatic actuators (SPAs) and an embedded range-sensor (RS) package. The soft layer and frame were selected to reproduce an antrum-like segment with a 50 mm relaxed diameter.
  • Modeling: RiSPA is modeled as a decoupled set of mass–spring–damper (MSD) elements (one per SPA), producing a state-space representation suitable for linear control design and offline LQR synthesis.
  • Control: An offline linear–quadratic regulator (LQR) static gain is used to track desired occlusion profiles. Simulations and experimentally applied control signals demonstrate regulation to 40%, 60%, and 80% occlusion with low error.
  • Validation: Experimental setup with proportional valves, pressure sensors, and an electromagnetic tracker was used for calibration and validation. Results show good agreement with simulations: occlusion errors ≤ ~1.5%–1% for larger occlusions and acceptable repeatability across repeated trials.
  • Applications: Biomimetic stomach/intestine simulators, peristaltic pumping, soft grippers and bio-inspired actuators where large radial contractions and embedded sensing are required.
