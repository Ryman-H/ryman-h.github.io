Bellows-Based Soft Actuator with Embedded Sensing

Soft actuation · Embedded sensing · Closed-loop soft robotics

A modular soft bellows actuator that integrates deformation and interaction sensing directly into its structure. The system is designed as a building block for soft robots that require large, controllable motion combined with real-time proprioceptive feedback, enabling adaptive and closed-loop behaviour without external sensing.

  • Problem: Soft pneumatic actuators provide compliant motion but typically lack integrated sensing, limiting controllability and robustness during interaction with objects or humans.
  • Approach: Design a bellows-style soft actuator that produces large axial deformation while embedding stretchable sensors within the bellows walls or along its surface to measure deformation, contact, and loading.
  • Validation: Bench-top characterisation of pressure–displacement behaviour, sensor response during cyclic actuation, and interaction experiments demonstrating repeatable sensing under large strains.
  • Outcome: A sensorised soft actuator capable of self-sensing its state, suitable for closed-loop control, adaptive interaction, and integration into larger soft robotic systems.

Applications

  • Soft grippers and adaptive end-effectors
  • Wearable and assistive soft robotics
  • Physical human–robot interaction and haptics
  • Experimental platforms for embodied intelligence

Design directions

  • Bellows geometry used as a mechanical gain to amplify motion and sensing resolution.
  • Embedded sensors positioned in high-strain regions to maximise signal quality.
  • Sensor signals used for simple closed-loop strategies (pressure modulation, contact detection).
  • Modular design allows scaling to multi-bellows arrays or integration into larger systems.
