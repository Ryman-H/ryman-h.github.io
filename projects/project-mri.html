MR-Safe Pneumatic Stepper for Needle Insertion

Ziting, L., Hashem, R., et al. - IROS24

Development of a compact, MRI-compatible pneumatic stepper actuator and manipulator for precise percutaneous needle insertion. The system is designed to operate safely within magnetic resonance environments, providing accurate insertion, rotation and depth control while avoiding electromagnetic interference with imaging.

  • Problem: MRI-guided percutaneous interventions remain limited by the lack of compact, accurate, and fully MR-safe robotic systems capable of operating within the confined geometry of clinical MRI bores without degrading image quality.
  • Approach: Design of a table-mounted, MR-safe parallel robot based on dual robotic arches and a scissor-folding mechanism, enabling five degrees of freedom for needle positioning using pneumatic actuation and non-magnetic materials.
  • Validation: Experimental evaluation demonstrated mean positioning errors of 3.14 mm for the robotic arch and 2.23 mm for the full needle insertion system, with MRI compatibility tests showing negligible image artefacts during static and dynamic operation.
  • Application: MRI-guided abdominal percutaneous interventions, including biopsy and ablation procedures for liver and kidney tumours, where accurate needle placement and radiation-free imaging are critical.
