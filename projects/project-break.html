Harnessing Symmetry Breaking in Soft Robotics: A Novel Approach for Underactuated Fingers

R. Hashem, T. Howison, A. Stilli, D. Stoyanov, W. Xu, F. Iida — IROS 2024

This work investigates how underactuated soft ring actuators (RiSPA) can be made to perform robust in-hand manipulation by intentionally harnessing symmetry breaking and self-organisation. Instead of fighting buckling and morphological nonlinearities, the method uses them: a ring of soft bellows (fingers) exhibits coordinated buckling that can be guided by frequency, duty cycle, and small perturbations to rotate an object clockwise or counterclockwise using a single pressure input. The approach is validated in simulation and with physical experiments showing repeatable object rotation and clear operating envelopes for frequency and pressure.

  • Problem: Controlling multi-degree, soft inflatable actuators for in-hand manipulation is difficult because compliance and buckling create many degrees of freedom and unpredictable behavior with standard control strategies.
  • Approach: Use a ring-shaped soft pneumatic actuator (RiSPA) and exploit symmetry breaking and self-organisation. By selecting amplitude, frequency and duty cycle of the pressure signal (and applying a brief perturbation), the actuator’s natural buckling causes coordinated finger motion that rotates objects with minimal explicit control. A simplified Simulink model (prismatic + rotational joints) and MATLAB parameter identification are used alongside empirical tests.
  • Validation: Buckling tests (cantilever model), pressure thresholds (Table I), simulation sweeps over frequency/duty cycle, and physical experiments demonstrate reliable CCW rotation without perturbation and controllable CW rotation after a perturbation (optimal perturbation ≈ 2 Hz, 70% duty).
  • Key results: The actuator achieved sustained rotations (12 rotations in 25s CCW) with ≈5% simulation/experiment error for CCW, and CW rotations were achievable post-perturbation (with larger discrepancy attributed to friction and tuning). Object shape affects repeatability (cylindrical ≈90% repeatability; square ≈40%).
  • Domain / Applications: Soft manipulation, underactuated hands, surgical/medical robotics testbeds, dexterous soft grippers, and bio-inspired robotic systems.
