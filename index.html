About

I am a Senior Research Fellow at the University of Oxford working on embodied intelligence in robotic systems. My research explores how physical embodiment through material properties, compliance, sensing, and morphology which can be co-designed with control and learning to produce adaptive, intelligent behaviour in real-world environments.

Rather than treating intelligence as a purely computational problem, my work investigates how intelligence can emerge from interaction between a robot’s body and its environment. I study soft and underactuated robotic systems in which mechanical structure and physical dynamics actively shape perception, control, and decision-making, reducing reliance on centralised control and large-scale data.

A central theme of my research is the use of application-driven systems as experimental testbeds for fundamental questions in embodied intelligence. Through platforms such as biomimetic organ phantoms, sensorised soft actuators, and wearable robotic devices, I examine how sensing, actuation, and physical interaction can be integrated directly into robotic bodies to support closed-loop, adaptive behaviour under uncertainty.

While many of these systems are motivated by healthcare and human-centred robotics, the underlying principles generalise to broader challenges in robotics, including resilient manipulation, physical human–robot interaction, and intelligent machines operating in unstructured environments. My work combines theoretical insight, computational modelling, and large-scale physical experimentation, with a strong emphasis on reproducibility and translation beyond controlled laboratory settings.

Research Directions

Embodied Intelligence

Investigating how intelligence in robotic systems can emerge from physical embodiment, by designing morphology, materials, and compliance so that a robot’s body actively shapes perception, control, and adaptive behaviour with reduced reliance on central computation.

Application-Driven Platforms

Developing application-driven robotic platforms inspired by human organ function,combining biomimetic soft actuators, physical robotic systems, and simulation with finite element analysis to study closed-loop behaviour, physical interaction,and performance in realistic, task-driven settings.

Human-Centred & Translational Robotics

Translating principles of physical interaction and system design into safe, resilient human–robot interaction, with a focus on wearable and protective robotic systems for injury prevention, clinical training, and operation in unstructured environments.

Teaching & Supervision

I view teaching as a core responsibility of academic practice and as a natural extension of research in robotics and embodied intelligence. My teaching is grounded in project-driven and laboratory-led learning, where students integrate theory, computation, and physical experimentation by designing and building real systems.

Across undergraduate, master’s, and doctoral levels, I emphasize experimental intuition, system-level thinking, and independent problem formulation. Many supervised projects are closely aligned with ongoing research and have resulted in peer-reviewed publications at leading robotics venues.
